CIRCUMSTANCES OF DISCOVERY

At approximately 20:47 PM on Friday, March 15, 2024, the Riverside Police Department received a 911 call from one EDGAR MOLE, age 62, employed as a night janitor at the Riverside Museum of Art and Antiquities. Mole reported discovering a female individual unresponsive on the floor of the East Gallery (Gallery 7), which houses the museum's permanent collection of Late Victorian decorative arts.

Units 4 and 7 responded to the call. Sergeant Diana Calloway arrived on scene at 20:58 PM and confirmed that the individual — subsequently identified as ELEANOR VOSS, age 54, Chief Curator of the museum — was deceased. The Riverside County Medical Examiner was contacted at 21:03 PM.

Mole had begun his evening cleaning rounds at approximately 20:30 PM, starting in the west wing as is standard procedure. He states he entered Gallery 7 at approximately 20:32 PM (his log corroborates this, as his cleaning cart was found positioned at the gallery entrance) and discovered the body near the display case containing the Ashworth Victorian silverware collection, positioned approximately eleven feet from the east corridor entrance.

---

SCENE DESCRIPTION

Gallery 7 (East Gallery) is accessed via two points: the central atrium and the east corridor. The east corridor runs from the staff stairwell adjacent to the acquisitions office block on the second floor down to the ground level, connecting to Gallery 7's side entrance.

Upon arrival, Sergeant Calloway observed the following:

1. ELEANOR VOSS was found lying face-down approximately three meters from the base of the Victorian silverware case. She was wearing business attire: a charcoal blazer, white blouse, gray trousers, and low heeled shoes. No defensive wounds were immediately apparent to the responding officer, pending medical examiner assessment.

2. A significant amount of blood was observed beneath the victim's head consistent with head trauma. The injury appeared to be to the posterior-left of the skull.

3. A bronze decorative bookend (subsequently logged as EXHIBIT 1-A) was found on the gallery floor approximately ninety centimeters from the body. The item appeared to be displaced from the victim's office on the second floor — a matching bookend of identical design was later identified on the victim's office desk (see Forensic Report FR-2024-0315). The bronze bookend showed visible reddish-brown smearing consistent with possible blood transfer, pending laboratory analysis.

4. No signs of forced entry to the museum. The museum's main public entrance had been locked by security personnel at 18:00 PM per standard closing procedure (security log confirms). Staff access via badge-swipe entry remained active for museum employees after hours.

5. The east gallery appeared otherwise undisturbed. No display cases were broken, no artworks appeared displaced, and no signs of robbery were evident.

6. A second-floor window in the acquisitions office block was found open approximately six inches, though this is reported to be a ventilation habit in that office area and may not be significant.

---

INITIAL WITNESS INTERVIEWS (PRELIMINARY)

Two individuals were located on museum premises at time of police arrival:

1. EDGAR MOLE, night janitor — provided the 911 call and discovery account. Cooperative. Remained on scene for initial statement.

2. TOBIAS REYES, museum security guard, shift 18:00 – 02:00 — located in the staff break room on the ground floor at time of police arrival. Reyes stated he had been on his scheduled 30-minute break commencing approximately 20:15 PM.

Museum records indicate that a third staff member, MARCUS BELLWEATHER (Head of Acquisitions, second floor office), had been working late this evening. His access badge record is being reviewed as part of the investigation.

---

INITIAL OBSERVATIONS AND NOTES

Time of death is pending medical examiner determination. Based on body temperature and rigor mortis onset observed by the ME upon arrival at 21:23 PM, preliminary assessment places likely time of death between 19:30 PM and 21:00 PM, with the narrower probable window being 19:45 PM to 20:30 PM.

The location of the body (east gallery) and the apparent weapon (acquisitions office bookend) suggest the possibility that the victim was moved or that the incident originated in the second-floor office area and the victim or suspect moved to, or through, the east corridor afterward.

The east gallery's connection to the staff corridor, specifically the corridor running from the acquisitions department's second-floor offices to the ground floor, is noted as potentially significant.

Investigation is ongoing. Physical evidence has been collected, tagged, and transferred to the RPD Forensic Laboratory. Scene perimeter maintained until forensic sweep is complete.
