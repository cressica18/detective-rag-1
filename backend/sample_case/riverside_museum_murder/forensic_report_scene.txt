PART I — SCENE EXAMINATION

East Gallery (Gallery 7), Riverside Museum of Art and Antiquities, was
examined as a crime scene under the direction of Lt. Morris Greer and
Sgt. Diana Calloway. The scene perimeter was maintained from 21:00 PM
until forensic sweep completion at 00:45 AM (March 16).

Scene condition upon entry: Gallery 7 was otherwise undisturbed. No display
cases were damaged. No artworks displaced. No signs of robbery or secondary
criminal activity beyond the homicide itself.

EXHIBIT 1-A: BRONZE DECORATIVE BOOKEND
Location at recovery: Gallery floor, approximately 90 cm from decedent's body,
southeast of position.
Description: Bronze cast decorative bookend, Art Nouveau design, depicting
seated figure with scrollwork base. Mass: approximately 1.4 kg. Dimensions:
16 cm tall × 12 cm wide × 5.5 cm (base) × 3.8 cm (base depth).
Surface condition: Visible reddish-brown smearing on base and lower right edge.
Blood typing: Sample taken, blood group A-positive. Eleanor Voss's blood group:
A-positive (confirmed from medical records). DNA sample extracted, pending
NDIS lab comparison — preliminary match expected (not yet confirmed at time
of this report).

ORIGIN DETERMINATION:
A matching bookend of identical design was located on the desk of Marcus
Bellweather in the Acquisitions Office, second floor (Exhibit 1-B). Both
items are part of a matched pair acquired by the museum in 2021 as decorative
pieces for the Acquisitions department suite, allocated to Mr. Bellweather's
office per inventory records (see EVIDENCE INVENTORY EV-2024-0315). The
pair would normally be located together in Bellweather's office. The presence
of EXHIBIT 1-A in Gallery 7 requires explanation.

EXHIBIT 2: EAST GALLERY FLOOR SAMPLE
Location: Approximately 50 cm south of decedent's final position.
Description: Heel scuff marks in light floor dust, consistent with heel drag.
Direction: Consistent with body having been moved or having fallen from a
standing position onto the floor — not consistent with body having been
relocated from elsewhere.

EXHIBIT 3: ACQUISITIONS OFFICE — EVIDENCE SAMPLES
Location: Marcus Bellweather's office, second floor, Acquisitions Block.
Items sampled:
  3-A: Surface wipe of Bellweather's desk surface — trace blood detected.
       Volume insufficient for typing at current stage. Luminol reaction
       confirmed at desk edge area (partial reaction, requires further analysis).
  3-B: Keyboard surface — luminol trace reaction (subsequent cleaning indicated).
  3-C: Matching bookend (EXHIBIT 1-B, still in office) — no blood trace.
       Confirms Exhibit 1-A was removed from office separately.

EXHIBIT 4: WASTE BIN — ACQUISITIONS OFFICE
Contents: Shredded paper fragments (financial documents, partially legible —
see attached inventory), one crumpled sheet, one paper towel with
apparent reddish-brown smear — positive luminol reaction. Indicates possible
clean-up activity in this office prior to police arrival. Paper towel sent
for blood typing. Preliminary: A-positive.

PART II — TRACE EVIDENCE SUMMARY

Fibers recovered from victim's blazer (shoulder area): Dark fabric, woven
cotton blend. Consistent with typical office/suit jacket material.
Comparison sample pending against garments of persons of interest.

Glass fragments: None.
Fingerprints, EXHIBIT 1-A: Partial latent prints recovered (two). One print
insufficient for comparison. Second print submitted to AFIS — no hit at
initial search. Re-examination with alternative light source ongoing.

Hair: Single dark hair recovered from victim's right shoulder (bagged as
EXHIBIT-HAIR-001). Does not match victim's hair. DNA analysis ordered.

PART III — DIGITAL AND ELECTRONIC EVIDENCE

The forensic team reviewed the victim's work laptop (found on her desk in
the Curator Suite, second floor). The laptop was logged into at 18:47 PM
per system event log, with the last save event at 19:12 PM. The document
open at last save was a confidential internal audit document titled:
"Acquisitions Review — Discrepancy Flag — March 2024."

This document is being reviewed by the financial crimes unit in conjunction
with the homicide investigation. Summary: The document contained a
detailed notation of apparent inconsistencies in acquisition cost records
associated with three recent purchases, flagging possible procurement fraud.
The notation referenced the Head of Acquisitions by title (not by name in the
recovered partial text) and stated the author intended to escalate the findings
to the Board of Trustees on Monday, March 18.

---

PART IV — SUMMARY OF KEY FINDINGS

1. EXHIBIT 1-A (bronze bookend) originated from Marcus Bellweather's office
   and is the probable murder weapon.
2. Trace evidence in Bellweather's office (blood luminol positive, cleaned
   paper towel) suggests the office may have been the primary crime scene
   or site of post-incident activity.
3. Victim's laptop contains a document flagging financial discrepancies
   connected to the Acquisitions department — establishing potential motive.
4. DNA and confirmatory analysis are pending; this report represents
   preliminary forensic findings.
