[RECORDING BEGINS]

DET. HARTE: Mr. Bellweather, thank you for coming in. For the record — this is a voluntary interview. You're not under arrest, you're free to leave at any time. Your attorney is present. Do you understand?

MARCUS BELLWEATHER: Yes. Yes, I understand. I want to cooperate fully. Eleanor was — I've worked with her for seven years. This is devastating.

DET. MEHTA: We appreciate that. Let's start with Friday evening. Can you walk us through your day at the museum on March 15th?

BELLWEATHER: Yes. I was in all day. I had — there were acquisition review meetings in the morning, internal meetings. Lunch at my desk. In the afternoon I was reviewing loan agreements for the autumn exhibition. Eleanor — Ms. Voss — she stopped by my office around 4:30 in the afternoon. We had a brief discussion about the autumn exhibition budget. It was entirely professional, nothing unusual.

DET. HARTE: And after that? 

BELLWEATHER: I continued working. I stayed late — that's not unusual for me, I frequently work late on Fridays. I was finalizing a loan agreement with the Hargrove Foundation.

DET. HARTE: What time did you leave the museum?

BELLWEATHER: I left at — it was around 7:45 PM. Quarter to eight. I packed up my desk, took my briefcase, and left through the staff exit on Mercer Street.

DET. MEHTA: The staff exit on Mercer Street. That requires a badge swipe to exit?

BELLWEATHER: That's right.

DET. MEHTA: And you used your access badge to exit?

BELLWEATHER: Yes, of course.

DET. HARTE: Mr. Bellweather, were you at any point in the east gallery or east corridor after — let's say after 7:00 PM?

BELLWEATHER: No. My office is on the second floor of the acquisitions block. I went from my office to the stairwell, to the ground floor, and directly out the Mercer Street exit. I didn't go near the galleries.

DET. MEHTA: Did you interact with Ms. Voss after the 4:30 PM meeting?

BELLWEATHER: No. I assumed she left before me, she usually does. She has a standing arrangement — had — dinner with her sister on Friday evenings, I believe. I was surprised to hear she was still in the building.

DET. HARTE: You mentioned your 4:30 meeting was about the autumn budget. Was there anything contentious about that conversation?

BELLWEATHER: No, not particularly. Budget discussions always involve some negotiation.

ATTORNEY CRANE: Is there a specific question here, Detective?

DET. HARTE: We have some questions about the acquisitions records. Ms. Voss had raised some concerns recently — are you aware of that?

BELLWEATHER: [pause] I'm not sure what you're referring to. If Eleanor had concerns about acquisitions records, that would ordinarily come to me directly. She hadn't raised anything formally.

DET. MEHTA: Informally?

BELLWEATHER: I'm — no. Nothing specific.

ATTORNEY CRANE: Mr. Bellweather, you don't have to speculate.

BELLWEATHER: No, nothing. Not specifically.

DET. HARTE: Going back to your departure time — 7:45 PM. Are you confident about that?

BELLWEATHER: Yes. Around 7:45.

DET. MEHTA: Not later?

BELLWEATHER: No. Around 7:45. I may have walked around for a few minutes, you know, gathering things, but I left around 7:45.

DET. HARTE: We may need to speak with you again as our investigation develops, Mr. Bellweather. For now, that's all.

BELLWEATHER: Of course. Whatever I can do.

[RECORDING ENDS — 14:47 PM]
