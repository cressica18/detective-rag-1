STATEMENT OF EDGAR THOMAS MOLE (in his own words, transcribed verbatim)

I come in every Friday night at half past eight in the evening — that's my regular shift, has been for eleven years. I park in the staff lot off the side entrance on Mercer Street, badge myself in through the staff door, and I pick up my cart from the storage closet on the ground floor just inside. That's the routine.

Tonight — tonight was March 15th, a Friday — I came in and badged in at 8:30. I know it was right about 8:30 because I was a few minutes late; the bus was delayed and I'd checked my phone while I was waiting at the stop. The badge reader will show you the exact time.

I started my rounds in the west wing, like I always do. Gallery 1, Gallery 2, Gallery 3. Nothing unusual in the west wing. Mr. Reyes — that's the security guard, Tobias — I passed him near Gallery 3 around — I'd say around 8:35, maybe 8:40. He was walking back toward the staff corridor, heading toward the break room I'd assume. He said hello, I said hello, that's all. Nothing unusual about him.

Then I worked through the central atrium and started into the east wing. Gallery 5, Gallery 6, Gallery 6 was fine. Then I pushed my cart through to Gallery 7.

When I came in through the main atrium entrance of Gallery 7 — not the corridor side, the wide atrium doors — I saw her. I could see her from the doorway. She was on the floor, over near the silverware case, face down. I thought at first maybe she'd fainted or taken ill. I called out — "Hello? Are you alright?" — no response. I walked across to her, quick as I could. When I got close I could see the blood on the floor beneath her head and I — I knew then. I've never seen anything like that. I called 999 — no, I mean 911, I called 911, immediately. I did not touch anything else. I stayed right there next to her without touching her, just as they tell you to do, until the officers arrived.

The time I found her — it was around 8:32, based on my cart log. We keep a sheet on the cart, you timestamp each gallery when you enter, health and safety requirement. I'd entered Gallery 5 at 8:30, Gallery 6 at 8:31, so Gallery 7 would have been 8:32. That matches. The 911 call was at 8:34, I believe, you can check that.

The gallery looked — strange, now that I think about it. I didn't notice at the time, I was in shock. But there was something — there was a scuff mark on the floor, a long one, between the corridor entrance at the side and where she was lying. Like something had been dragged, or maybe her heels, she'd been moved. I don't know. I just remember noticing the floor looked wrong. But I can't swear to it. I was very upset.

I did not see anyone else in the east wing. I did not hear any footsteps or voices. The east corridor side entrance of Gallery 7 — the door was closed but not locked, I noticed, because I usually prop it when I clean. When I went to prop it I realized it wasn't fully latched; I had to pull it slightly to close it properly. I don't know if that's normal.

Miss Voss — I knew her, of course. She'd been here longer than me nearly. Lovely woman. Always said good evening when she worked late, which she did often. She was very dedicated. If something happened to her in this building — I hope you find who did it.

I have nothing more to add.
