[RECORDING BEGINS]

DET. HARTE: Tobias, thank you for coming in. For the record — this is a
voluntary interview. You're not under arrest. You understand that?

TOBIAS REYES: Yeah, yes. I understand. I want to help. It's — yeah.

DET. MEHTA: Take your time. Let's start with your Friday evening shift.
What time did you come in?

REYES: My shift starts at six. I came in right around six, maybe just after.
I checked in at the security station.

DET. HARTE: And what does your shift involve?

REYES: I do the rounds. Walk each gallery floor, check the fire exits, check
the display cases are locked. On Friday evenings after public close at six,
I also do the final gallery check and log it. Then I'm at the station most of
the time. I do rounds every hour.

DET. MEHTA: Did you see Ms. Voss that evening?

REYES: I saw her around — probably about quarter past six? She came down from
upstairs and walked through the atrium. She said good evening. That was the
only time I saw her after the museum closed.

DET. HARTE: And Marcus Bellweather?

REYES: I know Marcus — Mr. Bellweather. I saw him earlier, like around seven.
He was coming down the stairs, I think — no, wait. I didn't see him. I heard
footsteps on the east corridor stairs around seven, maybe seven fifteen. I
assumed it was one of them — Ms. Voss or Mr. Bellweather. But I didn't see who.

DET. MEHTA: You didn't see who it was?

REYES: No. I was at the station. I heard footsteps. I didn't go check because
there were only two people still in the building and both are allowed to be
there. It's not unusual.

DET. HARTE: Let's talk about your break. Your records show a scheduled break
at 8:15 PM?

REYES: 8:15, yeah. That's my standard mid-shift break. Thirty minutes. I was
in the break room.

DET. HARTE: From 8:15 to 8:45 approximately?

REYES: Yes. Give or take a couple of minutes.

DET. MEHTA: Did you hear anything during that break? From the direction of
Gallery 7 or the east corridor?

REYES: The break room is at the back of the ground floor, west side. Gallery 7
is east. I don't — you can't really hear from there to the break room. It's a
big building. No, I didn't hear anything.

DET. HARTE: When did you first become aware something was wrong?

REYES: When Edgar — Mr. Mole — came running to find me. He was — he looked
awful. He said there was a woman on the floor in Gallery 7. I radioed it in
immediately. I went to Gallery 7 and I saw Ms. Voss. I could tell she was
— I didn't touch anything. I called 999. I mean, 911.

DET. MEHTA: Did you touch anything in the gallery?

REYES: No. Nothing. I stayed at the door. I didn't go in past the threshold.

DET. HARTE: One more thing. The badge records show that Marcus Bellweather's
badge was used to exit the Mercer Street staff exit at 8:22 PM — that's after
your break started. Did you see or hear anything around that time?

REYES: 8:22? No. I was in the break room. I didn't see him leave. But — that
would put him still in the building when I was on break, right? He told us he
left at quarter to eight. That's what I heard him tell someone — the other
detective — earlier. If the badge says 8:22, then...

DET. MEHTA: Let's not speculate, Tobias. Is there anything else you think is
relevant to tell us?

REYES: I don't think he did it. Marcus. He's always been professional. But
I'm telling you what I know. I know what the records say. He was here later
than he said.

[RECORDING ENDS — 11:02 AM]
