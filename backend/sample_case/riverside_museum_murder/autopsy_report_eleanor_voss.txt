PART II — INJURIES

INJURY 1 — PRIMARY AND LETHAL:
Location: Posterior-left cranium, specifically the left parietal-occipital region.
Description: Blunt-force impact, single point of contact, creating a depressed
stellate fracture of the left parietal bone with inward displacement of bone
fragments into the cranial vault. Maximum depression depth: 2.1 cm.
Underlying damage: Acute subdural hemorrhage with associated parenchymal
contusion. Hemorrhage volume estimated 85ml.

The injury pattern — stellate fracture with a defined, relatively narrow
contact area of approximately 6 cm × 4 cm — is consistent with a heavy,
smooth-edged object with moderate mass delivered in a single downward and
slightly lateral strike. The injury is not consistent with a fall; the
fracture geometry and depth indicate an actively applied force.

EXHIBIT 1-A CORRELATION: The bronze decorative bookend recovered from the
scene (logged as EXHIBIT 1-A, mass approximately 1.4 kg, base dimensions
approximately 5.5 cm × 3.8 cm) is consistent in dimensions and contact
characteristics with the injury. Trace material analysis is pending.

INJURY 2 — LACERATION, ANTERIOR LEFT FOREHEAD:
A secondary superficial laceration (3 cm) is present on the anterior left
forehead, consistent with contact with the gallery floor surface following
the primary blow. This is a post-collapse contact injury, not a second impact.

No other injuries identified.
